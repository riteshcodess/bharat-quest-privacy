Privacy & Data Safety Center

Bharat Quest is developed by Syntax Error with a local-first software architecture. This document outlines our data processing practices, Google AdMob advertising integrations, and user rights regarding on-device game storage.

01

Data Collection & Local Storage Architecture

Unlike cloud-dependent mobile applications that mandate account creation or background telemetry synchronization, Bharat Quest operates primarily as an offline-capable single-player application. We do not require email verification, passwords, or personal identity registration.

When you install and utilize the application, gameplay state is created, processed, and preserved exclusively within your device's internal application storage sandbox (`localStorage` and native Android data directory). This local repository includes:

Progression & Accuracy Metrics

Quiz completion records, score tallies, state-level mastery percentages, and historical accuracy across historical and mythological categories.

In-Game Economy & Streaks

Earned currency balances, daily login streak counters, daily challenge reward logs, and unlocked collectible legend cards.

Application Preferences

Locally configured audio settings, haptic vibration feedback toggles, and user-selected display identifiers.

Local-First Assurance

None of your on-device gameplay records, contact lists, media files, or personal identifiers are transmitted to our remote servers, analyzed for profiling, or transferred to data brokers.

02

Advertising Integration & Google AdMob

To maintain Bharat Quest as a free educational resource without subscription fees, the application integrates third-party advertisements delivered via the Google AdMob SDK (`@capacitor-community/admob`), operated by Google LLC.

When advertisement units—such as optional rewarded video placements or interstitial transitions—are requested and displayed, the Google AdMob network processes limited technical characteristics in accordance with industry standard advertising protocols:

  • Google Advertising ID (AD_ID): A system-assigned, user-resettable identifier utilized by Google for ad delivery, frequency capping (preventing repetitive ad displays), and ad attribution reporting.
  • Technical Device Specifications: Hardware configuration, Android OS release version, screen resolution parameters, system language, and network connection state.
  • Diagnostic & Performance Metrics: Anonymous telemetry regarding advertisement render failures or application exceptions encountered during ad loading.

User Control & Opt-Out: Users retain complete authority over personalized advertising. You may reset your device's Advertising ID or disable personalized ad delivery entirely through your system settings by navigating to Settings → Google → Ads on your Android device.

03

User Control & Data Erasure

Because application data resides entirely on your personal device rather than external servers, you exercise full administrative authority over your progression records at all times.

How to Reset or Erase Local Data

To permanently clear all gameplay data, open Bharat Quest, navigate to the Settings Sheet, and select the red Reset Progress button. This action immediately deletes all local save files from internal device memory, resetting the application to its initial state without requiring remote server verification.

Additionally, users who wish to create an external backup of their progression may utilize the in-app Download Save File feature to export a clean `.json` snapshot directly to their local documents storage.

Children's Privacy Compliance

Bharat Quest is designed as an educational exploration tool celebrating Indian cultural heritage suitable for general audiences. We do not knowingly solicit, collect, or store personal identity data from individuals under the age of 13.

Because the application functions without mandatory user accounts or centralized server databases, no names, birthdates, or contact details are ever collected from young players. Furthermore, advertising placements served via Google AdMob are configured in compliance with applicable child protection frameworks, including COPPA and the Google Play Families Policy requirements.
