Privacy Policy & Data Safety Declaration

This document details our data processing practices, offline local storage mechanisms, and third-party advertising frameworks for the Bharat Quest mobile application on Google Play. We adhere to data minimization principles and do not require remote server accounts or cloud data tracking.

Section 01

Information We Collect

Key Summary: Bharat Quest operates completely offline-first. All game progress, scores, and unlocked assets are created and stored locally within your device memory (`localStorage` and device storage). No personal gameplay data is uploaded to remote servers.

Unlike online multiplayer applications that require user registration, email verification, or continuous cloud synchronization, Bharat Quest functions autonomously on your mobile device. When you interact with the application, all progression metrics are written strictly to internal device memory.

The application locally generates and manages the following categories of data:

Quiz Performance Records

Historical accuracy across states, completed categories, best times, and XP progress.

Economy & Rewards

In-game coin balance, gem counts, daily challenge completion status, and login streak counters.

Unlocked Collection Items

Obtained legend cards, discovery notes, achievement badges, custom avatars, and selected home state.

Custom Configuration

Audio sound effects volume, background music toggle, haptic vibration preferences, and user display name.

Section 02

Advertising & AdMob Integration

To sustain free distribution of Bharat Quest on the Google Play Store, the application utilizes third-party advertisements delivered by Google AdMob (`@capacitor-community/admob`), an advertising service operated by Google LLC.

When advertisements are requested by the app, the Google AdMob SDK automatically collects and processes technical device identifiers and interaction metrics to serve relevant, frequency-capped ad units in compliance with Google's network policies.

  • Google Advertising ID (AD_ID): A unique, user-resettable identifier provided by the Android operating system. It is used exclusively by AdMob for ad attribution, ad frequency capping, and serving non-personalized or personalized ads depending on user consent.
  • Technical Device Metadata: Device manufacturer, model, operating system version, screen resolution, and network connection type (Wi-Fi vs. Cellular).
  • Diagnostic Crash Reports: Anonymous technical performance logs if an ad request fails or encounters a rendering error during playback.
Your Opt-Out Control: You can reset your Android Advertising ID or opt out of personalized advertisements at any time by navigating to your device settings under Settings → Google → Ads and adjusting your system-level privacy preferences.
Section 03

Local Storage & Data Deletion Controls

Because game progression is stored directly on your physical device rather than on centralized server databases, you maintain absolute authority and ownership over your data at all times. We provide direct in-app controls to manage, export, or erase your records:

In-App Data Reset

Navigate to the in-app Settings Menu and select Reset Progress. This action immediately and permanently clears all local storage data, resetting the game to its initial unplayed state.

Save File Export & Import

You may download a structured `.json` backup of your complete game progression directly to your local filesystem using the Download Save File option, or restore previously saved progress via Upload Save File.

Section 05

Third-Party Software Libraries

Bharat Quest incorporates validated third-party libraries to provide native mobile interactions and monetization frameworks. Each service operates under established legal privacy policies:

  • Google AdMob SDK (`@capacitor-community/admob`): Utilized for rendering advertisements. For comprehensive details regarding Google's data processing methods, review the Google Privacy Policy and How Google uses data from apps.
  • Capacitor Native Framework (`@capacitor/app`, `@capacitor/filesystem`, `@capacitor/haptics`): Utilized for managing background application state changes, local filesystem save operations, and haptic feedback responses.
Section 06

Children's Privacy & COPPA Compliance

Bharat Quest is an educational exploration game regarding Indian history and culture suitable for general audiences. However, we do not knowingly solicit, collect, or store personally identifiable information from children under the age of 13.

Because our application functions without user accounts, registration forms, or centralized databases, no personal identification data is ever collected from minor players. Additionally, our third-party advertising configuration via Google AdMob adheres strictly to applicable child protection regulations, including COPPA and the Google Play Families Policy where applicable.
