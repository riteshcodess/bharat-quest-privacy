Privacy & Data Safety Hub

Welcome to the official legal portal for Bharat Quest. We built this game with an offline-first philosophy: explore Indian heritage, collect rare cards, and master quizzes right on your device without intrusive tracking or mandatory accounts.

📅 Effective: July 19, 2026 🔒 Local-First Save Data 📢 Google AdMob Monitored
📦

Information We Collect

Unlike online multiplayer applications that mandate email verification, cloud account logins, or continuous background syncing to remote data centers, Bharat Quest is designed primarily as an offline-first mobile game.

When you launch and play Bharat Quest, all core gameplay data and progression metrics are created and stored directly inside your device's local memory (`localStorage` and native device file storage). Specifically, the app generates and maintains the following locally:

🏆

Quiz & Accuracy Records

Tracks your completed quizzes, scores, best accuracy per state, and historical performance across Indian history and mythology.

🪙

Economy & Streaks

Stores your earned coins, rare gems, daily login streak counters, and claimed daily challenge rewards.

🎴

Collection Inventory

Maintains your unlocked legend cards, state emblems, achievement badges, player avatars, and custom theme selections.

⚙️

App Settings

Saves your audio sound effects volume, background music toggle, haptic vibration feedback preferences, and chosen display name.

Local-First Guarantee: None of your personal identity, contact lists, photos, microphone recordings, or gameplay statistics are transmitted to our remote servers or sold to data brokers.

Advertising & Google AdMob

To keep Bharat Quest free to download and accessible across all Indian states and global regions, the application incorporates third-party advertisements delivered by Google AdMob (`@capacitor-community/admob`), a mobile advertising network operated by Google LLC.

When advertisements (such as optional rewarded video ads for extra game coins or interstitial breaks) are requested and rendered on your screen, Google AdMob automatically processes certain technical device identifiers and app usage metrics:

  • ✓
    Google Advertising ID (AD_ID): A unique, user-resettable identifier assigned by your Android OS. It is utilized exclusively for serving relevant ads, frequency capping (preventing repetitive ad views), and ad attribution.
  • ✓
    Technical Device Characteristics: Your device model, Android OS version, screen resolution, system language, and network connection status (Wi-Fi vs. Cellular data).
  • ✓
    Ad Diagnostic & Crash Metrics: Anonymous performance reports if an ad fails to load or causes an unexpected application freeze during rendering.

Your Opt-Out Control: You can reset your Android Advertising ID or opt out of personalized advertisements at any time by navigating to your phone's Settings → Google → Ads and adjusting your device-level privacy controls.

🗑️

Storage & Data Deletion

All game progression data is stored locally inside your application's secure data directory on your Android device. We do not maintain centralized user databases, nor do we require you to register an account with an email address or password.

Because you are in complete control of your local data, you have complete authority to manage, export, or permanently erase your progress whenever you choose right from within the app:

⚠️

In-App "Reset Progress"

Open the in-app Settings Sheet and tap the red Reset Progress button. This instantly and permanently wipes all local storage, resetting your game to zero immediately.

📂

Native Save File Export

Download a backup `.json` file of your complete game progress straight to your phone's Documents folder anytime using the Download Save File option.

Children's Privacy

Bharat Quest is an educational exploration game celebrating Indian culture and history suitable for general audiences. However, we do not knowingly solicit, collect, or store personally identifiable information from children under the age of 13.

Because the game operates without user registration or server-side accounts, no names, birthdates, or contact details are ever collected from young players. Furthermore, third-party advertising via Google AdMob is configured to adhere to applicable child protection regulations including COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) and the Google Play Families Policy where applicable.
